Campaigned as a Tech President and then forgot

Barack Obama campaigned as the tech president, but he chose Wheeler, he chose Genachowski and he was on the wrong side on SOPA. His administration and political party have done nothing on the issues most important to the Internet.

  • Net Neutrality. Picked two bad FCC chiefs and threatened to gut net neutrality.
  • High-skill immigration reform. Never happened.
  • SOPA. Democrats almost passed it.
  • NSA surveillance. Expanded, tarnishing US tech industry.

Now, President Obama’s choice for FCC chairman–a former cable & telco industry lobbyist–is threatening to gut net neutrality. The President and his party's inaction could risk us losing the open Internet as we know it. With the coming election threatening to oust democrats out of Congress, President Obama is desperately fundraising for campaign donations for his party. This is the time for the President to prove himself to the Internet before the end of this election cycle.

This is our question to the President: Do you want to do the right thing for the Internet and protect it for generations to come or lose the mid-term election for your party AND your legacy on technology?
